IMPACT OF COVID-19 ON UN SDG11: SUSTAINABLE CITIES AND COMMUNITIES


Decreasing air pollution world-wide is crucial for reaching the UN Sustainable Development Goals. Air pollution is estimated to cause seven million deaths globally each year1. Air pollution has also been attributed to reduced vegetation health and crop yields2. Air quality is assessed by levels of: Ozone, Nitrogen Dioxide, Sulphur Dioxide, PM2.5 and PM10 and carbon monoxide.

COVID-19 has caused drastic reductions in transport and energy production causing reductions in air pollution. This can be seen in the map providing carbon monoxide (CO) levels in 2019 and 2020.

The short-term reductions in air pollution caused by COVID-19 highlights the need for improved policy, energy production and green transport to sustain reduced air pollution in the long term.
